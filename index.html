Frequently Asked Questions

Why are oceans fundamentally important to global survival?

Oceans cover over 70% of the Earth's surface, provide more than half of the atmospheric oxygen supply through phytoplankton, cycle global thermal dynamics, and directly support billions of planetary residents.

What is the primary target objective of SDG 14?

UN Sustainable Development Goal 14 seeks to prevent and drastically reduce marine pollution of all kinds, sustainably manage coastal ecosystems, minimize acidification metrics, and end overharvesting by enforcing strict international maritime frameworks.

Why are marine species becoming endangered at accelerated rates?

Anthropogenic interaction—specifically accidental bycatch, chemical habitat toxicosis, corporate reef destruction, and warming temperature bands—disrupts breeding timelines faster than wildlife groups can naturally recover.

How does microplastic tracking alter food chain safety?

Large plastics break down into microscopic particles that absorb surrounding chemical toxins. These are consumed by lower-tier marine organisms, leading to bioaccumulation that compromises food safety across the human consumption grid.
